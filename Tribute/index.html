Nikola Tesla

Genius Inventor and Engineer

Nikola Tesla (1856-1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist best known for his contributions to the development of alternating current (AC) electrical systems.

Tesla's inventions and discoveries laid the foundation for modern electrical power systems. His work with AC power was essential for the Second Industrial Revolution, powering the world's industries, machinery, and homes.

Among his many inventions, Tesla developed the Tesla coil, induction motor, and contributed to the development of X-rays. His vision included wireless communication and free energy for the world.

Despite facing financial struggles during his later years, Tesla's impact on science and technology is immeasurable. He left behind a legacy that continues to inspire inventors and engineers to this day.
